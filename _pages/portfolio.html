Research Highlights

Peer-reviewed and submitted work in the mesosphere–lower thermosphere, observed with meteor radars.

1

Diurnal and Seasonal Variations of Meteor Velocity Observed at Middle Latitude by the Mengcheng Meteor Radar

Wu et al. · Submitted to JGR: Space Physics · Oral, 20th National Symposium on Solar-Terrestrial Space Physics

Key Points

  • The azimuth of maximum meteor velocity rotates clockwise, from north (local morning) to south (local evening).
  • The velocity distribution has two Gaussian peaks at ~28 km/s and ~54 km/s, revealing bi-annual and annual variations respectively.
  • Major meteor showers produce 45–97% higher counts than the background, driving the velocity peaks upward.
2

Climatology of the Global Mesospheric Diurnal Tides Observed by Meteor Radars

Yi et al. (incl. Wu, K.) · Submitted to Geophysical Research Letters

Key Points

  • The global diurnal tide climatology is resolved with fifteen meteor radars across the MLT region.
  • Diurnal tides are strong at low and mid-latitudes (10–50°N/S), and weak near the equator, higher-mid and polar latitudes.
  • The height of maximum tidal amplitude tracks the solar zenith angle at northern low and mid-latitudes.
3

A Summary Report on Space Physics Practical Education

Keywords

Practical education · Space Physics · Chinese Meridian Project

4

Perturbations from the 2022 Hunga-Tonga Eruption in the Mesosphere–Lower Thermosphere

Wu et al. · WACCM-X simulation & meteor-radar observations · Oral, 4th CGS Workshop · Poster, AGU 2023

Key Points

  • Wave perturbations from the eruption are tracked across four radar stations by projecting winds toward the great-circle direction from the epicenter.
  • Meteor-radar observations are compared directly against WACCM-X simulations in profile, amplitude, and wavelet space.
