## The Core Idea

进攻和退路都要有 — "you need both the attack and the retreat." The biggest mistake F1 founders make is going all-in on their startup without building a parallel path that protects their visa status. The second biggest mistake is being so cautious about the visa that they never actually build the company.

This guide is about doing both at the same time.

## What It Covers

- **Legal structure** — when you can incorporate, when you cannot, who should be on the cap table.
- **CPT and OPT founding** — using the work authorization you already have.
- **STEM OPT extension** — the 36-month runway and how to use it well.
- **H1B for founders** — yes, it is possible, and how it actually works.
- **O-1 for founders** — the criteria and the timeline.
- **Cap table and equity for non-citizens** — what looks normal to a US VC.
- **The fallback plan** — back to PhD, back to a big-company job, back to China.
- **Tax** — the parts that surprise founders, especially around equity.
- **Mental health** — visa anxiety is real and underrated.

## A Disclaimer

This is not legal advice. Visa law is fact-specific and changes. The guide is a starting point for *what to ask your immigration lawyer*, not a substitute for one.
