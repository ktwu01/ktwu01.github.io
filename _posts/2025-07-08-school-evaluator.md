## Why Rankings Are Not Enough

US News rank, QS rank, ARWU rank — they all collapse a complex institution into a single position on a list. That works as a first filter. It fails as a decision tool, because the variance *between students at the same school* is much larger than the variance between schools.

School Evaluator gives you the dimensions a ranking can't.

## What It Scores

- **Academic fit** — does the program actually teach what you need?
- **Career outcomes** — where do graduates from your specific track end up?
- **Cost vs. financial aid** — net cost after grants, not sticker price.
- **Location and quality of life** — climate, cost of living, city energy.
- **Network and alumni** — who you'll meet and who you'll know later.
- **Research opportunities** — labs, funding, advisor availability.
- **Visa and immigration support** — for international students.
- **Personal fit** — culture, pace, values.

Each dimension is weighted by you. The composite output is meaningful because it reflects *your* priorities, not US News's.
