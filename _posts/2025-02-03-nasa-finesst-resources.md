NASA's Future Investigators in NASA Earth and Space Science and Technology (FINESST) is one of the most underused fellowships among US graduate students. Most PhD students have never heard of it, and the ones who have often miss the deadline because the proposal expectations aren't obvious from the call alone. This repo is a curated guide of links, tips, and examples to help you write a competitive FINESST proposal.

## What Is FINESST

FINESST is NASA's graduate research fellowship program for Earth science, planetary science, heliophysics, astrophysics, and biological/physical sciences. A successful proposal funds up to three years of graduate research at the rate set in the call. The student is the PI on the grant, with their advisor as the formal sponsor.

If you are a US graduate student doing research in any of NASA's science divisions, FINESST is one of the highest-leverage fellowships you can apply for.

## Why This Repo Exists

When I first applied for FINESST, I could not find:
- Sample successful proposals to calibrate against.
- A clear breakdown of how each division (Earth, Planetary, Helio, Astro, Bio/Phys) reads proposals differently.
- A timeline that worked backward from the deadline.
- Advice on the science management plan, the part most students rush.

So I started collecting what I learned. The repo grew from there.

## What You Will Find

- **Official program links** — call documents, FAQ, ROSES.
- **Sample proposals** (where authors have made them public).
- **Section-by-section guidance** — significance, technical approach, science management plan.
- **Common reviewer complaints** — surfaced from public summary statistics.
- **Timeline templates** — working backward from the typical mid-cycle deadline.
- **Advisor coordination** — what your sponsor actually needs to provide.
- **Tips on reusing NSF GRFP material** if you've already written one.
