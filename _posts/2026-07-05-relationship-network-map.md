> **Goal**: Use a 4x3 grid to place every relevant person "by position, with a matching strategy," then review it regularly to maximize resource use and manage risk.

> Author: [Koutian Wu](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ktwu01/); [GitHub: ktwu01](https://github.com/ktwu01/)

---

## I. Two Axes

| Axis | Meaning | Criteria |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Closeness** | Frequency of interaction and emotional depth | **Core circle** (shared life or deep collaboration)<br>**Inner circle** (frequent collaboration or academically critical)<br>**Outer circle** (occasional collaboration, process-linked)<br>**Service circle** (transactional services, easily replaceable) |
| **Influence** | Whether they can "bottleneck" my academics / wealth / family | **High**: veto power, directly controls key resources<br>**Medium**: significantly helps or hinders, but can be routed around<br>**Low**: only affects efficiency or comfort; losses recover quickly |

---

## II. Building the 4x3 Grid

1. List every relevant person (family, partner, mentors, peers, service providers).
2. First sort by closeness, then by influence.
3. Each person gets exactly one cell, based on the most direct relationship.
4. Use short "verb + noun" labels for quick scanning.

## III. Action Strategy Template

| Cell | Key action |
| --- | --- |
| Core x High | Deepen trust; align on long-term goals; lead with win-win proposals |
| Core x Medium | Clarify division of labor; share information; give fast feedback |
| ... | ... (abbreviated) |

---

## IV. Continuous Iteration

* **Quarterly review**: Update each person's cell position and log events.
* **Risk scan**: Check whether any high-influence cell shows early signs of conflict.
* **Opportunity scan**: Who in the outer circle deserves promotion to the inner circle?

---

## V. Tooling Suggestions

First, don't overcomplicate it — writing it down by hand with a simple method works best. Tools come second.

* **Notion / Obsidian**: Tables plus bidirectional links.
* **Figma / Whimsical**: Visualize the relationship map.
* **Calendar reminders**: Schedule the regular review.

---

## Conclusion

Managing your network with a structured lens is how you find the path of least resistance and maximum synergy across a complex academic-to-industry career.
