## Why Picking an Advisor Is the Real Admission Decision

Most prospective PhD students obsess over the school name. The students already inside the program know that the lab and the advisor matter ten times more. A great university with a hostile advisor is a five-year prison. A modest university with a generous advisor is an accelerator.

Advisor Calculator turns that intuition into a checklist.

## What It Scores

- **Funding stability** — does the lab have grants that cover your full timeline?
- **Publication record** — first-author opportunities for students, not just the PI.
- **Graduation track record** — do students finish, and how long does it take?
- **Mentorship style** — hands-on vs. hands-off, and which suits you.
- **Group culture** — collaborative or competitive, healthy or toxic.
- **Career outcomes** — where do alumni go, and is that where you want to go?
- **Power dynamics** — visa sponsorship, authorship norms, conflict resolution.

You weight each dimension yourself. The output is a single composite score plus a per-dimension breakdown so you can see exactly where the risks are.

## Who This Is For

- Prospective PhD students choosing between offers.
- Master's students considering a PhD with a specific advisor.
- Postdocs evaluating a new research group.
- International students for whom advisor-driven visa risk is real.

## How to Gather the Data

The calculator is only as good as your inputs. The repo's README points you at the questions to ask current students, alumni, and the PI themselves. The single highest-signal source is **a 30-minute call with a senior student in the lab**, ideally without the PI's knowledge.

## Tech Stack
