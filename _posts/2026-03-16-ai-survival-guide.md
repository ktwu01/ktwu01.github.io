Every knowledge worker is now competing with an LLM for some part of their job. AI Survival Guide is a handbook for figuring out which parts, what to do about it, and how to come out the other side better at your craft instead of replaced by it.

## The Premise

The "AI will take your job" framing is wrong in a useful way. The accurate framing is: **AI will take the boring 60 percent of your job, and you will be expected to do twice as much of the interesting 40 percent at higher quality**. Either you adapt to that, or you get squeezed out.

This guide is for the adapters.

## What It Covers

- **What LLMs are good at** — and how to recognize when you're being out-performed.
- **What LLMs are bad at** — and how to lean into those skills.
- **Tooling and workflows** — practical setups that compound your output.
- **Skills to invest in** — judgment, taste, system design, communication.
- **Skills to deprioritize** — anything that has become a commodity.
- **Career strategy** — moving up the value chain rather than across it.
- **Mental health** — coping with constant capability shock.

## Who This Is For

- Engineers, designers, writers, analysts, researchers, and lawyers who feel the squeeze.

- Managers trying to advise their teams without sounding like a press release.

## A Note on Tone

This is not an "AI will save us" pamphlet and it is not a doomer essay. It is a practical document about staying useful when the ground keeps moving. Plan for the realistic case, not the marketing case.
